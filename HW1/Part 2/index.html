New York City

Quick Facts:

Founded: 1624

Population: 8.336 Million (2022)

Boroughs: Manhattan, Brooklyn, Bronx, Queens, Staten Island.

More than 800 languages are spoken.

United Nations headquarters were established here after WWII.

Famous Sites: :

Statue of Liberty: An Iconinc display of freedom located on Liberty Island. It was built in 1886 as a gift from the French. Regarded as one of the most recognizable figures in American History.

Times Square: One of the most vistied places in not only the country, but the entire world. Known for the huge ads and a place people flock to for the ball drop, a yearly tradition signifying the beggining of the new year.

Wall Street: Located in the financial district in Lower Manhattan. The street is home to global financial markets, major banks, investment firms, etc. The streets name is often associated with wealth, economic power, and financial controversy during financial crises

Education:

Colombia University: Est. 1754. Apart of the prestigious Ivy League. Well renowned university knon for it's research and innovation. The journalism school is known for it's work in journalism education and shaping modern journalism.

New York University: Est. 1831. Features the highly regarded Stern School of Business. Well known for it's finance and business education, as well as it's connections to Wall Street.
